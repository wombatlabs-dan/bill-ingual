# Bill-ingual

## The Problem
Americans are drowning in financial documents they don't understand. 53% of homeowners are blindsided when their mortgage payment suddenly spikes due to escrow adjustments. 80% of medical bills contain errors. Only 27% of adults can pass a basic financial literacy test. Yet the companies sending these documents — mortgage servicers, hospitals, insurers — offer zero explanation. Consumers are left Googling, calling hold lines, and overpaying bills they can't decode.

## The Solution
Upload any confusing financial document — a mortgage statement, medical bill, insurance EOB, or tax notice — and get a plain-language explanation of what it says, why you should care, and exactly what to do about it.

## Target Customer
First-time homebuyers (median age 40), adults managing medical bills (79M Americans), and anyone navigating financial paperwork without a financial advisor. They're on Reddit's r/personalfinance and r/FirstTimeHomeBuyer, Bogleheads forums, and Googling "why did my mortgage payment go up" and "how to read a medical bill."

## Market Evidence
- Bogleheads has a recurring thread titled "Why is there always an escrow shortfall?"
- One Wells Fargo customer reported their payment jumped from $1,222/month to $1,950/month overnight due to a $7,500 escrow shortage they didn't understand
- 40% of American adults find medical bills confusing; 29% don't understand what they're being billed for
- 50% of consumers avoid seeking medical care entirely due to billing confusion
- $88 billion in medical debt on credit reports — much of it disputed or misunderstood

## Competitive Landscape
- **Monarch Money / Copilot Money**: Track spending but don't explain documents
- **CoPatient / Resolve**: Human-powered medical bill audits; slow, expensive, 35% cut of savings
- **NerdWallet / Credit Karma**: Generic financial education; don't look at YOUR specific document
- **Better.com "Betsy" / Chestnut (YC)**: AI mortgage assistants for the lending process, not post-close statement explanation
- **Gap**: No consumer product takes a specific document you're confused by and gives you a personalized explanation of what it means, why it matters, and what to do

## Market Opportunity
- 79M Americans with medical bill problems + ~2M first-time homebuyers/year + ~130M adults below financial literacy proficiency
- CoPatient charges 35% of savings (~$1,050/bill); BillFixers charges $24.99/month — most consumers spend time, not money, because no affordable tool exists
- Freemium model: 3 free documents, then $4.99-$9.99/month
- Market is growing: healthcare costs rising, property taxes increasing, financial products getting more complex, and the post-Mint vacuum left millions without financial visibility

**Killer stat**: "79 million Americans are confused by their medical bills. 53% of homeowners don't understand why their mortgage payment just went up. We built the financial translator they've been Googling for."

## The Big Bet
We're betting that a single, well-crafted AI interaction — upload a document, get a three-layer explanation (What does it say? Why should I care? What should I do?) — can replace hours of Googling, hold-line calls, and financial anxiety. The companies that send you these documents will never explain them. We will.
