# Bill-ingual — Pitch Script (3 Minutes)

**Presenter Notes:** This is a 3-minute hackathon pitch. You're telling a story, not reading a report. The show-of-hands opening replaces Slides 1 and 2 — you do it BEFORE clicking to any slide, or with just the title slide up.

---

## [OPENING — Show of Hands] ~30 seconds

*[Title slide up, or no slide yet. You're standing in front of the audience.]*

> "Quick show of hands — how many people here have a mortgage?"
>
> *[hands go up]*
>
> "Keep your hand up if you've ever been surprised by a payment increase you didn't expect."
>
> *[some hands stay]*
>
> "OK. New question — how many of you have ever received a medical bill and thought... I have no idea what this means or if it's even correct?"
>
> *[lots of hands]*
>
> "That's pretty much everyone. You're not alone — **$88 billion** in medical debt is sitting on credit reports right now, and **60%** of homeowners are surprised when their mortgage payment changes. We built **Bill-ingual** — the financial translator you've been Googling for."

## [SLIDE — The Solution] ~20 seconds

*[Click to Solution slide]*

> "Bill-ingual gives you three answers for any financial document you upload:
>
> **What does this say?** Plain English. No jargon.
> **Why should I care?** What changed and what it means for your wallet.
> **What should I do?** A dispute letter, a payment recommendation, or just reassurance.
>
> One upload. Three answers. Let me show you."

## [SLIDE — Mortgage Demo] ~30 seconds

*[Click to mortgage demo slide, or switch to live demo]*

> "Sarah and Michael just bought their first home. They get a three-page letter saying their payment is jumping from $1,800 to $1,987. It's full of escrow projections and RESPA disclosures. They don't even know what escrow *means*.
>
> Bill-ingual reads it and starts with the basics: *'Escrow is a savings account your lender manages to pay your taxes and insurance. Your county reassessed your home's value up 18%, so your taxes went up. That's why your payment increased.'*
>
> Then it recommends which payment option to pick and tells them they can file a free property tax protest. That's not fighting back — that's *understanding*."

---

## [SLIDE — Medical Bill Demo] ~30 seconds

*[Click to medical bill demo slide, or switch to live demo]*

> "Now the medical bill. Maria goes to the ER for an ankle injury. She gets a bill for **$1,400**.
>
> Bill-ingual cross-references the bill with her insurance EOB and catches something: her actual responsibility is only **$340**. She's being **overcharged $1,060**.
>
> The hospital billed at full price instead of the negotiated insurance rate. Bill-ingual catches the discrepancy and generates a dispute letter. That's $1,060 back in her pocket."

## [SLIDE — Market + Competitive Gap] ~25 seconds

>

>
> **Nobody** takes YOUR document and gives you a personalized explanation with next steps. That's the gap."

## Tips for Delivery

- **Opening:** The "keep your hand up" transition is the key move. It keeps momentum and physically commits the audience. Make eye contact while hands are up — let the moment breathe.
- **Opening (stats):** You only drop two numbers: $88B and 60%. That's it. The audience already proved the problem with their hands — the stats just confirm it.
- **Solution:** Speed through this. It's setup for the demos. Don't linger.
- **Mortgage demo:** Slow down on "they don't even know what escrow means." That's the relatable moment. The "understanding, not fighting back" line is the key differentiator from the medical bill demo.
- **Medical bill demo:** Hit "$1,060" hard. Pause after it. Judges remember dollar amounts.
- **Close:** The killer stat callback lands because the audience already raised their hands at the beginning. It feels like a full circle.
- **General:** You have 30 seconds of buffer. Use it for a natural pace rather than rushing. If you finish at 2:30, that's perfect — it shows confidence.
